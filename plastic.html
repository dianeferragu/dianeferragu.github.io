Microplastics Research

My research investigates the accumulation of microplastics in human biological systems and their impact on health. By leveraging machine learning models such as Random Forest and XGBoost, I analyze correlations between chemical class, age at exposure, and tissue retention levels.

This study identifies key risk factors, with findings suggesting that younger individuals and endocrine system exposure have higher susceptibility to microplastic toxicity. The goal of this research is to bridge computational biology and environmental health science, informing policies on plastic exposure risks.

Microplastic Composition

This analysis categorizes different plastic types found in biological tissues. Some polymer classes accumulate more frequently than others, indicating selective retention mechanisms within organs.

Feature Importance

Machine learning models rank age at exposure and chemical class as the most significant predictors of microplastic accumulation, highlighting the need for targeted studies on vulnerable populations.

Model Accuracy

A confusion matrix validates the predictive accuracy of AI models in classifying microplastic exposure effects, reinforcing their potential for biomedical risk assessment.

Microplastics Frequency in Organs

The frequency analysis of microplastics across different organs reveals patterns of accumulation, with certain biological systems being more prone to retention. This data informs further toxicological studies.
